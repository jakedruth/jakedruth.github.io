Keeper

Time to make:
8 months
Team Size:
8 people
Game Engine:
Unity
Description:

My Capstone Project, Keeper. Keeper is an educational game that teaches the player about bees and pollination. A lot of effort went into making this game fun to play, both the mechanics and the story. My job on this project was to work on implementing the animations the artist created, iterating on the various systems and managers like saving and loading, creating an editable journal, and finding and fixing bugs. This game was worked on over the course of two semesters as our group's capstone project with a little over 1900 hours of work and effort.

(re)Pair

Time to make:
48 hours
Team Size:
2 people
Game Engine:
XNA
Description:

The Global Game Jam theme for 2020 was repair, a theme all about fixing stuff. Our team decided to interpret the theme as pairing up things again. (re)Pair is a puzzle game about Noah's ark breaking down and having all of the animals get loose. It is up to the player to re-pair all of the escaped animals, again. This is done by pushing the same breed of animals together, but watch out! Some animals just can't fight their urge to eat smaller prey. Avoid bringing tasty snacks near these animals, like crocodiles and snakes. Other animals just don't want to move. They would much rather wait around until food comes to them than be pushed by the likes of you. You will come across several challenging puzzles and find various pick-ups along the way.

Gridlock Wars

Time to make:
2 months
Team Size:
2 people
Game Engine:
XNA
Description:

This game was created for my AG 131 Final in 2014. It was one of my first major games made in XNA. My partner and I split the work, where I focused on the grid and particle effect sytem. The game is based of the popular game Geometry Wars. It plays fairly similar in the sense that the players try to survive for as long as possible and getting a highscore. It can support up to 4 players simultaneously, and requires an xbox controller for each player.

Two Minutes To Live

Time to make:
1 month
Team Size:
2 people
Game Engine:
Unreal Engine 4
Description:

This game Was Created in the Unreal Engine 4. This was our first game in the new engine so it was mostly a learning experience as to how UE4 worked. The concept of the game is that the player has only 2 minutes to beat the final boss, but they are extreamly under powered. However, they can restart the game when ever they want taking back any money they have found. On this project, I focused on the player class and the restarting of the world while keeping abilities and money.

Invisimaze

Time to make:
1 month
Team Size:
1 person
Game Engine:
Flash/ ActionScript 3.0
Description:

This was originally made during the 2014 Global Game Jam durring the last 10 hours. The theme was "We don't see things as they are, we see them as we are." I decided to make a game where depending on which direction you were going, the maze would show different things. Since the game jam, I have added small things to the game, including a level select screen, but no major game concepts have been changed.

The screen shot shows the concept of the game. Only one colored wall will be visible at any time, in this case green. All other walls will be invisible while they are still physically there. The object is to navigate the maze and get to the Magenta goal block as quick as possible, with the fewest amounts of keyboard presses, and without dying.
